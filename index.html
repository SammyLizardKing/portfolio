PROFILE_PHOTO.JPG

Sam Euntaek Kim

I design architecture as a medium for narrative, memory, and encounter — where the overlooked and "in-between" conditions often carry the most meaning. My work moves between storytelling and systems, using computation and AI as tools to question authorship and expand how form can be generated and read. Across projects — from playful worlds built out of fragmented memories to sectional studies of performance spaces — I'm interested in how spatial experience shapes behavior, atmosphere, and social life. I also explore how material and built form can act with agency, producing enchantment not through spectacle, but through emergent interactions.

[ 01 ] OUROBOROS
4A POSITIONS · FA2025 [ + ]
TYPE.............INSTITUTIONAL EXTENSION
SITE...............7TH & SANTA FE, LOS ANGELES
STUDIO............4A DESIGN STUDIO: POSITIONS
INSTRUCTOR.......RUSSELL THOMSON
TOOLS.............RHINO · GRASSHOPPER · AI

My project for an addition to SCI-Arc begins with an observation. The building we occupy now is a quarter mile long linear structure. It produces an excessive amount of circulation along its length, so much that I believe circulation is actually the most important program in the school. The necessity to walk the length of the building, whether it be long or short, enables exchange, visual and verbal. In a sense, circulating along the line promotes multiple forms of interaction and discourse, from the formal to the informal.

This proposal works again with a linear form, but instead of simply doubling or paralleling the existing building, it begins to loop back into itself, creating not only a line but a place. Along this loop, segments sometimes separate into autonomous bars, and at other moments they draw together, merging into wider, laminated volumes. As a series of loops they form circuits, minimizing the liability of dead ends.

"Rather than accepting one definitive ground line, the new bar allows the ground to shift. As it bends, dips, and lifts, the project produces sections where ground is no longer singular: at moments the bar hovers above grade, at others it sinks into it, and sometimes the ground passes through the building as circulation without entering its interior."

[ 02 ] NEW MARIINSKY
3B ASSEMBLIES II · SP2025 [ + ]
TYPE.............PERFORMING ARTS CENTER
SITE...............ST. PETERSBURG, RUSSIA
STUDIO............3B DESIGN STUDIO: ASSEMBLIES II
INSTRUCTOR.......ERIC OWEN MOSS
CONCEPT.......... DEFORMATION / COLLISION

Envisioning the extension as a punch — a forceful collision — one might expect the deformation of the New Mariinsky to be unpredictable and chaotic, like a box absorbing an impact. To mediate this chaos and introduce architectural coherence, I developed a folding technique. The posture of the new building emerges from a diagram generated by this fold: a grid is applied to a folded sheet of paper, and upon unfolding, the distortions of the grid yield a diagram of deformation. This serves as both a record of impact and a generative tool — translating the violence of collision into a spatial language of articulated folds.

The proscenium of the Old Mariinsky extends across the canal, transforming into a bridge that connects to the new site. The bridge culminates in a deliberate "punch" into the New Mariinsky — the result of an assemblage of agents, the historical and the contemporary Mariinsky, converging through a shared architectural and performative interface.

Influenced by Jane Bennett's concept of material vitality, the architecture is understood as responsive rather than passive — engaged in a dynamic interaction between two entities, each with agency.

[ 03 ] LA APARTMENT
3A ASSEMBLIES I · FA2024 [ + ]
TYPE.............RESIDENTIAL + COMMERCIAL
SITE...............HIGHLAND PARK, LOS ANGELES
STUDIO............3A DESIGN STUDIO: ASSEMBLIES I
INSTRUCTOR.......DAVID FREELAND
TOOLS.............RHINO · AUTOCAD · GRASSHOPPER

Plaid is a pattern created by overlaying two or more grids with consistent intervals. We adapted this concept to divide our site evenly into five parcels — not by depicting an image of plaid but by embracing its generative process. By superimposing two grids derived from the surrounding urban fabric, we forged connections between different urban contexts while directly engaging with the field conditions.

Massing was generated through an algorithm that autonomously aggregated blocks. Influenced by parametricism, this process removed direct control, avoiding arbitrary placements while embracing computational logic. The parcel lines stem from overlapping tilted grids, while the massing inclines sequentially from an orthodox grid. Each element aligns with the previous one, gradually deforming the system while maintaining a 50/50 balance of solid and void.

The elevation features a striking composition of metallic panels juxtaposed with matte finishes, creating a dynamic contrast that accentuates the geometric volumes — establishing an alternative expression of solid and void.

[ 04 ] 92ND ST SCHOOL
2A WORLDS I · FA2020 [ + ]
TYPE.............ELEMENTARY SCHOOL
STUDIO............2A: ARCHITECTURE OF EDUCATION
INSTRUCTOR.......KAREL KLEIN
PARTNER.......... MATTHEW SCHOLTZ
CONCEPT.......... UNFORGETTING MEMORIES

Memories are like scattered puzzle pieces, drifting through the labyrinth of our minds. Over time, they blur, fragment, and fade, shaped by entropy and distortion. Rather than vivid, concrete recollections, they exist in an abstract, elusive form — part reality, part fantasy.

The concept of the design is derived from a board game. The goal of the game is to connect grey areas, which implies enchanted space — and by playing the game, students experience exploration through an unknown world. I and Matt attempted to design a school that gives the same experience to the students. Students can explore through this half-real, half-unknown space and reach each classroom.

— ORDINARY DIORAMA: SIDEWAYS STORIES FROM 92ND ST SCHOOL —

Ordinary Diorama explores the hidden layers of school life — those unplanned moments and in-between spaces where a different kind of education unfolds. Beyond libraries and playgrounds, these shadowed spaces capture the raw, unscripted experiences of youth. This project challenges the idea that memories endure because of their perfection. Instead, it celebrates their authenticity.

[ 05 ] TERRA-ASSIMILATION
2B WORLDS II · SP2021 [ + ]
TYPE.............FILM STUDIO + FESTIVAL
SITE...............EAGLE MOUNTAIN, CALIFORNIA
STUDIO............2B DESIGN STUDIO: WORLDS II
INSTRUCTOR.......SOOMEEN HAHM
PARTNER.......... NICOLAS BARREDA

In this project, we were tasked with proposing a specific entity to coexist on the site alongside human inhabitants. Our objective was to explore the harmony and conflicts that would arise between these two entities, ultimately envisioning and portraying new environmental dynamics and typologies for the speculative near future.

The structures have been designed to symbolize various stages in an ecological succession, capturing the essence of both biological and geological processes. In order to mimic the unpredictability of these processes, we used a variety of modeling techniques that incorporated a certain degree of chance, giving us different iterations every time the methods were applied.

"Soon after, it began playing with the inorganic matter of the geological stratum, shifting plates of rocks and minerals, creating a fragmented monolith. Platforms rising and falling, buildings twisting and rearranging positions almost as fluidly and organically as the beds of plants nearby."
